## Notes on language choices

The Thai lines are Thai because they carry the channel's weight — สายไฟ, สายน้ำ, the river's name itself. These words have texture in Thai that English flattens.

The English lines are English because the images are universal and land harder in plain speech — "The lines on the map can be erased", "They came with maps and flags", "Soldiers stand on opposite banks."

The bilingual pairings (Thai line + English line) work as call and response — one voice from the bank, one voice from the water.

The Vietnamese outro line (*Chúng ta là một* — We are one) is the river speaking in the language of its delta, where it finally reaches the sea. One word from the far end of the Mekong, arriving last.

## Style Guide — Revised for Cyber Nagara

**Paste into Suno style field:**

`dark cinematic electronic, Thai traditional instruments processed through dark ambient, deep sub bass, slow build, cinematic drops, multilingual Thai and English, ancient future, atmospheric, 110 BPM`

**Production notes:**
- Intro: sparse — single processed khim, ambient river texture, spoken delivery
- Verse 1: khim melody, temple bells, sub bass low and distant. Vocals alternate Thai and English naturally
- Chorus: full hit — khim + ranat + sub bass. The emotional peak. Thai lines carry the weight
- Verse 2: saw duang enters, darker register. English lines are clean and hard against the Thai texture
- Bridge: energy drops. Almost spoken. Then rebuilds — this is the defiance moment
- Final Verse: vast and open. Ranat drops out. Khim alone over bass
- Outro: mantra fade. Thai first, then English, then Vietnamese — the river reaching the sea
