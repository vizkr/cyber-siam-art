**Prompt Notes / Structure Instructions:**
- **Intro:** Clean single-note guitar, almost hesitant. A *khim* (hammered dulcimer) plays, but it is heavily processed with long, dark delays and reverb, sounding like a radio signal bouncing off a satellite. No drums.
- **Verse 1:** Clean guitar, soft bass, no distortion yet. The delayed *khim* continues to weave through the vocal lines, feeling cold and mechanical.
- **Pre-chorus:** Distortion builds, rock drums enter. Industrial-sounding Thai percussion (*klong khaek* processed with heavy compression and slight distortion) joins the build.
- **Chorus:** Full heavy riff, Scorpions-weight guitar, dark synth underneath. A *ranat ek* (Thai xylophone) plays a rapid counter-melody, but it is pitched down an octave and run through a bitcrusher or distortion pedal, making it sound like a malfunctioning machine rather than a wooden instrument.
- **Verse 2:** Riff settles, half-clean. The delayed, ghostly *khim* returns, echoing the "radio theory" theme.
- **Pre-chorus 2:** Distortion builds again, industrial Thai percussion drives the rhythm forward.
- **Chorus:** Full heavy riff, dark synth, and the pitched-down, distorted *ranat ek* counter-melody.
- **Bridge:** Riff drops completely. Dark electronic pulse only, almost spoken delivery. A single, heavily synthesized and stretched note from a *pi nai* (Thai oboe) wails like a dying siren during the "wire above my head" line.
- **Final chorus:** Everything together — heavy guitar, rock drums, dark electronic pulse, industrial Thai percussion, and the frantic, distorted *ranat ek*. The biggest, most chaotic but controlled moment.
- **Outro:** Back to clean guitar only, fading out with a final, lingering, delayed strike of the *khim* that slowly degrades into static.
