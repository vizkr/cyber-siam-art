# Ghazal 184 — The Weight of Trust
### Völur Linnus Channel
### Hafez (14th Century) — Song Adaptation

---

## Structure & Concept

**The Dynamic:** A duet. A conversation between the human and the divine.
- **Male Voice (Farsi):** The poet, the madman, the one who bears the weight. Sung in the original 14th-century Persian.
- **Female Voice (English):** The response, the echo, the angel at the tavern door. Clean, modern English.

**The Instrumentation:** 
- Traditional Persian instruments: *santur* (hammered dulcimer), *ney* (end-blown flute), *daf* (frame drum).
- A single heavy electric guitar enters late (at the bridge) — representing the weight of the trust the heavens refused.

---

## Lyrics

**[Intro]**
*(Sparse santur playing a traditional Persian dastgah melody. A slow, rhythmic daf beat begins.)*

**[Verse 1 — Male, Farsi]**
دوش دیدم که ملایک در میخانه زدند
گل آدم بسرشتند و به پیمانه زدند
*(Dush didam ke malayek dar-e meykhane zadand)*
*(Gel-e Adam besereshtand o be peymane zadand)*

**[Chorus 1 — Female, English]**
Last night the angels came to the tavern door
They took the clay of Adam and poured it on the floor
They sat with me, the beggar, in the ecstatic light
And drank the wine of heaven in the middle of the night

**[Verse 2 — Male, Farsi]**
آسمان بار امانت نتوانست کشید
قرعه کار به نام من دیوانه زدند
*(Aseman bar-e amanat natavanest keshid)*
*(Qore-ye kar be nam-e man-e divane zadand)*

**[Chorus 2 — Female, English]**
The heavens could not carry the weight of the trust
The mountains and the oceans turned away in dust
The burden was too heavy for the angels to claim
So they cast the lot of madness, and it landed on my name

**[Verse 3 — Male, Farsi]**
جنگ هفتاد و دو ملت همه را عذر بنه
چون ندیدند حقیقت ره افسانه زدند
*(Jang-e haftad o do mellat hame ra ozr beneh)*
*(Chun nadidand haqiqat rah-e afsane zadand)*

**[Bridge — The Shift]**
*(The traditional instruments drop out. A single, heavy, distorted electric guitar enters, playing a slow, crushing riff. The daf is replaced by heavy rock drums.)*

**[Female, English — over heavy guitar]**
Forgive the seventy nations and the wars they fight
They couldn't see the truth, so they made up the light
Forgive the seventy nations and the blood they spill
They couldn't see the truth, so they kill and they kill

**[Verse 4 — Male, Farsi — over heavy guitar]**
آتش آن نیست که از شعله او خندد شمع
آتش آن است که در خرمن پروانه زدند
*(Atash an nist ke az shole-ye u khandad sham)*
*(Atash an ast ke dar kharman-e parvane zadand)*

**[Final Chorus — Male and Female together, Farsi and English overlapping]**
**Female:** The fire that makes the candle smile is not the real flame
**Male:** آتش آن است که در خرمن پروانه زدند
**Female:** The real fire is the one that burns the moth's harvest away
**Male:** قرعه کار به نام من دیوانه زدند
**Female:** They cast the lot of madness, and it landed on my name

**[Outro]**
*(Heavy guitar fades out. The santur returns, playing the intro melody. The male voice speaks the final couplet softly.)*

کس چو حافظ نگشاد از رخ اندیشه نقاب
تا سر زلف سخن را به قلم شانه زدند
*(Kas cho Hafez nagoshad az rokh-e andishe neqab)*
*(Ta sar-e zolf-e sokhan ra be qalam shane zadand)*

*(Fade to silence)*

## Suno Prompt (Custom Mode)

**Style tags:**
`traditional Persian instruments, santur, ney, daf, male Farsi vocals, female English vocals, duet, slow build, single heavy distorted electric guitar entering late, doom metal bridge, mystical, atmospheric, 14th century poetry`

**Production Notes:**
- The Farsi verses must retain the rhythmic cadence of the original poetry (the *zadand* rhyme).
- The English choruses should be clean, melodic, and slightly ethereal.
- The transition at the bridge must be jarring — the ancient acoustic world suddenly crushed by the weight of the modern electric guitar. This is the "weight of trust" arriving.
