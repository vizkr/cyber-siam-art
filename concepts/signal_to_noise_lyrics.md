# Signal to Noise
### Alex Vizcroft — Pure English, Classical Guitar meets Heavy Metal & Thai Instruments

---

**[Intro — solo classical guitar, nylon string, intricate Segovia-style fingerpicking, delicate and precise. A single, processed khim (hammered dulcimer) note echoes in the background]**

---

**[Verse 1 — classical guitar continues, soft vocal]**
My father's hand upon my shoulder
The water running by the door
She looked away as I grew older
Three weeks until I joined the war
I left the quiet of the morning
I left the only life I knew
Without a sound, without a warning
They broke me down to build me new

---

**[Pre-chorus — classical guitar abruptly stops. Heavy, distorted metal riff crashes in, accompanied by driving rock drums and industrial Thai percussion]**
They stripped the name right off my chest
They took the boy and killed the rest
This body isn't mine to keep
But something woke while I was asleep

---

**[Chorus — full heavy metal riff, Scorpions-weight, dark synth underneath. A distorted, pitched-down ranat (Thai xylophone) plays a rapid counter-melody]**
I didn't come here for the fighting
I came to find what I had lost
Inside the static and the lightning
I found the signal and the cost
In the equations and the silence
There is a truth beneath it all
A quiet hum beneath the violence
I hear the frequency call

---

**[Verse 2 — heavy riff drops out. Return to the intricate classical guitar, but now it plays a darker, more complex melody. The delayed khim weaves through the notes]**
Page forty-seven, radio theory
The numbers started making sense
My eyes were red, my mind was weary
But I could see through their defense
The drugs, the books, the late-night reading
They built the scaffold in my mind
I saw the very thing I'm needing
The thing I left so far behind

---

**[Pre-chorus 2 — classical guitar stops. Heavy distortion and industrial percussion return]**
Not in a church, not on a hill
But in the math, the quiet thrill
The thing beneath the world we see
It was always waiting there for me

---

**[Chorus — full heavy metal riff, dark synth, distorted ranat counter-melody]**
I didn't come here for the fighting
I came to find what I had lost
Inside the static and the lightning
I found the signal and the cost
In the equations and the silence
There is a truth beneath it all
A quiet hum beneath the violence
I hear the frequency call

---

**[Bridge — all instruments drop out except a dark electronic pulse. Vocal is almost spoken. A wailing, synthesized pi nai (Thai oboe) sounds like a dying siren]**
Seven years gone. I came back to the water.
My father older. She was still waiting.
But I brought something back with me.
Something that has no name.

The wire above my head... I could hear it singing.

---

**[Guitar Solo — classical guitar and heavy distorted electric guitar trade phrases, battling each other, before harmonizing together over the heavy rhythm section]**

---

**[Final Chorus — biggest moment. Heavy guitar, classical guitar flourishes, dark synth, industrial percussion, and frantic ranat all playing together]**
I didn't come here for the fighting
I came to find what I had lost
Inside the static and the lightning
I found the signal and the cost
In the equations and the silence
There is a truth beneath it all
A quiet hum beneath the violence
I hear the frequency call

---

**[Outro — heavy instruments fade out abruptly. Only the solo classical guitar remains, playing the delicate intro melody, fading into static]**
*The signal was always there. Beneath the noise.*
